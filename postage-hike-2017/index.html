POSTAGE HIKE 2015

Charity Hike for DC Books to Prisons


Sunday, August 9, 2015 at 11 a.m.

Grosvenor/Strathmore Metro Station — Meet by the bus bay, right outside the station at the newspaper bins.

Questions and optional RSVP to btopdc@gmail.com

Come out for a special charity hike to raise postage money for DC Books to Prisons. The event will consist of a short hike, an Indian buffet lunch, and a trip to a used bookstore.

Meet at 11 a.m. just outside the Grosvenor/Strathmore Metro Station on the Red Line, Rockville/North Bethesda, MD. Note that Metro runs less frequently on the weekend and is sometimes delayed due to track work.

Total distance will be about 5 miles. Please ensure that you are capable of handling 5 miles. This is a point-to-point hike. We will walk from the metro to Rock Creek Trail, and head north, ending up at Mem-Sahib Indian Restaurant where we'll indulge in a lunch buffet, suitable for both herbivores and carnivores. Other options exist if Indian food is not your thing. After eating, we will adjourn to the Friends of the Montgomery County Library Bookstore, a book lover's paradise. Finally, we will walk to White Flint Metro Station. If you drive, you can take the metro one stop south (or you can redo the hike in reverse on your own). Predicted end time is around 3:15.

DC Books to Prisons is the beneficiary of this hike. Most funds raised by this charity are used for postage costs. Hundreds of requests pour in each month; the biggest limiting factor is postage money. The suggested donation amount is $5, which is the cost of sending 3 pounds of books to a prisoner.

**Please bring a donation for DC Books to Prisons, your SmartTrip card, cash for lunch ($13 + tax, tip, extras), and water. If you would like to make a donation online instead of bringing cash, that is fine. You can also make your donation in the form of self-adhesive postage stamps. Stamps worth $1, $1.20, or $2 are especially appreciated.
