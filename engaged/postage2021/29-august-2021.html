--- title: "Postage Rates, Effective 8/29/2021" ---

[Last updated: July 20, 2021]

USPS is expected to increase its postage rates on August 29, 2021. There is some uncertainty as to the details, and we will not be confident of the new rates until a volunteer mails some test packages on August 30. However, the following reflects the best information currently available to us.

Note 1: The underlying rule beneath the rates listed above is that the first pound costs $3.19 and each additional pound costs 63 cents.

Note 2: In the table above, the third column shows suggested stamp combinations for using up leftover stamps.com postage that was printed at the January 2021 rates. These combinations involve overpaying for 2-pound packages by 3 cents. HOWEVER, if we buy 40-cent stamps (which are technically "postcard forever" stamps) before the rate increase, they will cost us only 36 cents each.

First-Class Mail

Letter-sized envelopes weighing up to one ounce will cost 58 cents to mail. Large envelopes (called "flats" by USPS) weighing up to one ounce will cost $1.16 (two forever stamps) to mail. Additional ounces will continue to cost 20 cents regardless of the size of the envelope.

Note: Do not overstuff letter-sized envelopes. Letter-sized envelopes should weigh less than 3.5 ounces and be less than 1/4 inch thick. Exceeding these limits can cause an envelope to be considered a "parcel" rather than a "letter", resulting in drastic increases in cost.

The simplest way to pay for first-class mail is to apply the appropriate stamp(s) for the first ounce (i.e. one forever stamp for a letter-sized envelope or two forever stamps for a large envelope) then apply as many "additional ounce" stamps as are needed. Priority Mail

According to stamps.com's blog, USPS didn't ask the Postal Regulatory Commission for permission to increase Priority Mail rates, but "This could change as additional USPS announcements are expected".
