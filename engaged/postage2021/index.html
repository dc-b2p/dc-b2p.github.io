[Last updated: January 26, 2021]

The following rates went into effect on January 24, 2021.

Note 1: The underlying rule beneath the rates listed above is that the first pound costs $2.89 and each additional pound costs 56 cents.

Note 2: In the table above, the third column shows suggested stamp combinations for using up leftover stamps.com postage that was printed at the 2020 rates. These combinations involve overpaying for 1-pound packages by 1 cent and overpaying for 4-pound packages by 2 cents.

First-Class Mail

Most rates remain the same. Letter-sized envelopes weighing up to one ounce continue to cost 55 cents, and large envelopes (called "flats" by USPS) weighing up to one ounce continue to cost $1. However, "additional ounces" now cost 20 cents regardless of the size of the envelope. (Previously, additional ounces cost 15 cents if the envelope was letter-sized and 20 cents if the envelope was large.)

Note: Do not overstuff letter-sized envelopes. Letter-sized envelopes should weigh less than 3.5 ounces and be less than 1/4 inch thick. Exceeding these limits can cause an envelope to be considered a "parcel" rather than a "letter", resulting in drastic increases in cost.

The simplest way to pay for first-class mail is to apply the appropriate stamp for the first ounce (i.e. a forever stamp for a letter-sized envelope or a $1 stamp for a large envelope) then apply as many "additional ounce" stamps as are needed. Priority Mail

There is uncertainty about the Priority Mail rates that just went into effect. Seemingly reliable sources have contradicted each other, and we will be confident of the new rates only after a BtP volunteer hands a Priority Mail envelope to a USPS clerk and is told how much to pay. However, the following information is probably correct.

Size Cost
