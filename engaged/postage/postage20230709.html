[Last updated: September 9, 2023]

USPS increased its postage rates on July 9, 2023. Below is information on the new rates.

The underlying rule behind the rates listed above is that the first pound costs $3.92 and each additional pound costs 75 cents.

First-Class Mail

Letter-sized envelopes weighing up to one ounce cost 66 cents to mail. Large envelopes (called "flats" by USPS) weighing up to one ounce cost $1.35 to mail. (Note: The new rate means that it is no longer possible to pay for a flat by using two forever stamps, as was the case in the past.) Additional ounces continue to cost 24 cents regardless of the size of the envelope.

Note: Do not overstuff letter-sized envelopes. Letter-sized envelopes should weigh less than 3.5 ounces and be less than 1/4 inch thick. Exceeding these limits can cause an envelope to be considered a "parcel" rather than a "letter", resulting in drastic increases in cost.

The simplest way to pay for first-class mail is to apply the appropriate stamp(s) for the first ounce (i.e. one forever stamp for a letter-sized envelope or two forever stamps plus a 3-cent stamp for a large envelope) then apply as many "additional ounce" stamps as are needed. Although BtP doesn't send postcards or international mail, we sometimes use "postcard forever" and "international forever" stamps. "Postcard forever" stamps are worth 51 cents and "international forever" stamps are worth $1.50.

We believe that all the information above is correct, but it is easy to make mistakes when dealing with long price lists. If you discover any inaccuracies in the information above, please alert both Jen and Maya immediately.

This page summarizes the rates that are relevant to us. If you would like to see exhaustive details on all the price changes USPS implemented, see USPS's 69-page price list in PDF format.
