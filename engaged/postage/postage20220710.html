--- title: "Postage Rates, Effective 7/10/2022" ---

[Last updated: June 23, 2022]

USPS is expected to increase its postage rates on July 10, 2022. In the past, we have frequently been given information on upcoming rate increases that turned out to be incorrect. Because of this, we will not have full confidence in the new rates until a volunteer mails some test packages on July 11. However, the following reflects the best information currently available to us.

First-Class Mail

Letter-sized envelopes weighing up to one ounce will cost 60 cents to mail. Large envelopes (called "flats" by USPS) weighing up to one ounce will cost $1.20 (two forever stamps) to mail. Additional ounces will cost 24 cents regardless of the size of the envelope.

Note: Do not overstuff letter-sized envelopes. Letter-sized envelopes should weigh less than 3.5 ounces and be less than 1/4 inch thick. Exceeding these limits can cause an envelope to be considered a "parcel" rather than a "letter", resulting in drastic increases in cost.

The simplest way to pay for first-class mail is to apply the appropriate stamp(s) for the first ounce (i.e. one forever stamp for a letter-sized envelope or two forever stamps for a large envelope) then apply as many "additional ounce" stamps as are needed. Although BtP doesn't send postcards or international mail, we sometimes use "postcard forever" and "international forever" stamps. After the rate change goes into effect, "Postcard forever" stamps will be worth 44 cents and "international forever" stamps will be worth $1.40.
