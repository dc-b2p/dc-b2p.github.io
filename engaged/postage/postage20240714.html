[Last updated: July 15, 2024]

USPS increased its postage rates on July 14, 2024. Below is information on the new rates.

The underlying rule behind the rates listed above is that the first pound costs $4.63 and each additional pound costs 75 cents.

First-Class Mail

Letter-sized envelopes weighing up to one ounce cost 73 cents to mail. Additional ounces cost 28 cents.

Large envelopes (called "flats" by USPS) weighing up to one ounce cost $1.50 to mail. In a striking change to past practice, additional ounces are now of variable cost, according to page 6 of USPS's 67-page guide to the new rates. The chart is reproduced below. (One volunteer has speculated that USPS's chart contains typos and will be corrected at some point. We shall see.)

Note: Do not overstuff envelopes. Letter-sized envelopes should weigh less than 3.5 ounces and be less than 1/4 inch thick. Flats should weigh less than 13 ounces and be less than 3/4 inch thick. Exceeding these limits can cause an envelope to be considered a "parcel" rather than a "letter" or "flat", resulting in drastic increases in cost.

To pay for letter-sized envelopes, apply one forever stamp and the appropriate number of extra-ounce stamps. To determine the cost to mail a large envelope ("flat"), refer to the chart below.

Note: This means that any large envelope weighing more than 12 ounces would be cheaper to send by Media Mail than by First-Class Mail. (Unfortunately, many large envelopes are ineligible for being mailed this way, but a few are eligible for being sent by either method.)

Although BtP doesn't send postcards or international mail, we sometimes use "postcard forever" and "international forever" stamps. "Postcard forever" stamps are worth 56 cents and "international forever" stamps are worth $1.65.

We believe that all the information above is correct, but it is easy to make mistakes when dealing with long price lists. If you discover any inaccuracies in the information above, please alert both Jen and Maya immediately.

This page summarizes the rates that are relevant to us. If you would like to see exhaustive details on all the price changes USPS implemented, see USPS's 67-page price list in PDF format.
