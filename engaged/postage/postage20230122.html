--- title: "Postage Rates, Effective 1/22/2023" ---

[Last updated: January 18, 2023]

USPS will be increasing its postage rates on January 22, 2023. Below is information on the new rates that we believe to be correct. However, due to previous instances of USPS sources providing inaccurate information, we will lack full confidence in the rates listed below until a volunteer mails test packages on January 23. Please alert both Maya and Jen if you discover any inaccuracies below.

First-Class Mail

Letter-sized envelopes weighing up to one ounce will cost cost 63 cents to mail. Large envelopes (called "flats" by USPS) weighing up to one ounce will cost $1.26 (two forever stamps) to mail. Additional ounces will continue to cost 24 cents regardless of the size of the envelope.

Note: Do not overstuff letter-sized envelopes. Letter-sized envelopes should weigh less than 3.5 ounces and be less than 1/4 inch thick. Exceeding these limits can cause an envelope to be considered a "parcel" rather than a "letter", resulting in drastic increases in cost.

The simplest way to pay for first-class mail is to apply the appropriate stamp(s) for the first ounce (i.e. one forever stamp for a letter-sized envelope or two forever stamps for a large envelope) then apply as many "additional ounce" stamps as are needed. Although BtP doesn't send postcards or international mail, we sometimes use "postcard forever" and "international forever" stamps. "Postcard forever" stamps will be worth 48 cents and "international forever" stamps will be worth $1.45.

This page summarizes the rates that are relevant to us. If you would like to see exhaustive details on all the price changes USPS is implementing, see USPS's 69-page price list in PDF format.
