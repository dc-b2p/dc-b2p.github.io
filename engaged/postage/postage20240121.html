The underlying rule behind the rates listed above is that the first pound costs $4.13 and each additional pound costs 74 cents.

First-Class Mail

Letter-sized envelopes weighing up to one ounce cost 68 cents to mail. Large envelopes (called "flats" by USPS) weighing up to one ounce cost $1.39 to mail. (Note: It is no longer possible to pay for a flat by using two forever stamps, as was the case in the past.) Additional ounces continue to cost 24 cents regardless of the size of the envelope.

Note: Do not overstuff envelopes. Letter-sized envelopes should weigh less than 3.5 ounces and be less than 1/4 inch thick. Flats should weigh less than 13 ounces and be less than 3/4 inch thick. Exceeding these limits can cause an envelope to be considered a "parcel" rather than a "letter" or "flat", resulting in drastic increases in cost.

The simplest way to pay for first-class mail is to apply the appropriate stamp(s) for the first ounce (i.e. one forever stamp for a letter-sized envelope or two forever stamps plus a 3-cent stamp for a large envelope) then apply as many "additional ounce" stamps as are needed. Although BtP doesn't send postcards or international mail, we sometimes use "postcard forever" and "international forever" stamps. "Postcard forever" stamps are worth 53 cents and "international forever" stamps are worth $1.55.
