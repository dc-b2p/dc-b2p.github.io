New Fiscal Sponsor

In a recent newsletter we announced that we were transitioning to a new fiscal sponsor. We're delighted to announce that the transition is complete, and that we are now a fiscally sponsored project of Empowerment WORKS, a 501c3 nonprofit organization. Because of this, we are once again able to accept both online donations and checks. We look forward to a great relationship with Empowerment WORKS!

Another team continues with the neverending task of contacting prisons and departments of corrections to ensure that we have current information on the rules for sending books to the prisoners in their custody. We will continue doing what we can to serve prisoners while keeping our volunteers safe and complying with local laws on social distancing.

Being quarantined at home for an extended period has given some of our volunteers and supporters new insights into why certain types of books are frequently requested by prisoners. An article in The Washington Post titled "The 10 types of readers you meet during a quarantine" describes categories of readers such as "seize-the-day readers keeping busy with new skills" and "escapists running away to fantastical worlds". Of the latter type of reader, the article says that:

Readers are fleeing to appealing worlds that share little in common with our own, like J.R.R. Tolkien's Middle-earth and George R.R. Martin's Westeros. Hogwarts, too.

Just as described in the article, some of our volunteers and supporters have been escaping into fantasy or attempting to learn new skills while confined to home. One volunteer wrote that, "I had zero interest in fantasy before the coronavirus came, but am now obsessed with it because it provides my mind with a much-needed break from reality. For the first time, I understand why fantasy novels figure so prominently on our list of most-requested books. Being stuck in my apartment day after day without exercise is also making me understand why prisoners keep asking for books on bodyweight exercise." How Wikipedia Helps Us Serve Prisoners

We are blessed to have many volunteers who are deeply knowledgeable about books, and blessed also to have many books on our shelves. However, sometimes we lack either the knowledge or materials we need to serve a prisoner. A useful resource in these situations is Wikipedia.

How does Wikipedia help us? In several ways:

  • When we receive a letter asking for books by a particular author, and the volunteer reading the letter is unfamiliar with the author, Wikipedia provides a quick way to determine the correct genre. This greatly speeds up the process of finding suitable books.
  • When a volunteer is considering sending a particular book to a prisoner but isn't sure whether the book includes content that's banned in prisons, it's often possible to find a summary of the book on Wikipedia. There are, of course, many other web sites that also provide information on books, but many avoid spoilers. Wikipedia holds nothing back.
  • When an incarcerated reader requests books on an obscure non-fiction subject, a Wikipedia printout can be sent in lieu of a book. Because Wikipedia has articles in many languages, we can even use Wikipedia printouts when responding to requests from prisoners who have limited proficiency in English. Fortunately, Wikipedia allows text from its articles to be shared under a Creative Commons License, so we do not violate any copyright laws when creating printouts for incarcerated readers.

We are grateful for this resource that helps us meet the demand for educational materials in prisons.
