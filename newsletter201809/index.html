Prisoner Artwork





"Even though I just LOVE drawing action and sci-fi and fantasy super hero stuff, I do draw these more darker kind of drawings because that's what I feel like the times I'm depressed. And I am DEPRESSED here A LOT! I much rather draw happy fun stuff, but I can't help but feel trapped a lot of the times. The one I drew of me in my cell alone is how I feel some times... like a man trapped in a small box. And I NEVER can leave that box!... The larger drawing with me in my cell where I am not alone is how I feel other times, just me in my cell with my demons. I heard somewhere that we all have our demons and so this is me with mine. There's a bird flying out of my cell and it's supposed to be made out of pages from letters and pages from books because they take me out of my prison... I don't know if my art makes sense to you, but it makes sense to me. I just thought I'd share that with you."

Books for Children Visiting Incarcerated Family Members

Joe, Kim, and Graham met through their work with Reach Incorporated, an organization that trains teens to teach younger students. As committed supporters and regular volunteers, these three people came to know each other. And, over time, friendships developed.

Last year, Reach's founder, Mark, got married. Wanting to make a special gift, this trio of terrific people began thinking of ways to celebrate Mark's marriage to his wife, Elizabeth, in a way that was meaningful to all.

It was at that time that BtP received approval to distribute children's books to visitor waiting rooms in the federal prison system. Reach had facilitated the creation of children's books by teens in Washington DC, and all that was needed to distribute those books to prison waiting rooms was funding. Working with their publishing partners, Shout Mouse Press, the three friends made a gift to distribute Reach's books.

In the coming months, five of Reach's books will be distributed to all 120 federal prisons where DC citizens serve time -- 600 books in total! When children visit incarcerated family members, they will be able to read books authored by Reach teens. [** Check on verb tenses, since article was written a while ago. Has the distribution now taken place? **]

Joe will never meet the kids that read these books. Kim will likely never hear anyone thank her. Graham won't get to see the young readers enjoying the books. But, all the same, they chose to be there in a really powerful way for young people visiting loved ones in federal prisons. And, with their help, Reach and Shout Mouse will be able to bring comfort at a time when it's needed the most.
